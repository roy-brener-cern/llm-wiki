Mandate

The OAB is responsible for steering offline activities that affect at least two activity areas. Examples are

  • Define strategies and schedule for major software releases
  • Define strategies and schedule for data re-processing(s)
  • Define main strategies for Monte Carlo productions for any purposes, e.g. physics analyses, technical studies, upgrade studies (Note: the content and priorities of MC samples for physics analyses are defined by physics coordination)
  • Collect special requests for data taking from the Trigger, Data Preparation and Physics areas (e.g. special control/calibration samples), prioritize them and send them to the Run Coordinator.
  • Define strategies and schedule for large scale production of derived formats for physics analyses (e.g. DAOD production)
  • Define strategies for the long-term preservation of ATLAS data
  • Solve possible conflicts between the four areas (Trigger, Computing, Data Preparation, Physics): e.g. concerning trigger rates, data streams, ingredients for productions and Software releases, etc.

The OAB may form subcommittees or task forces. E.g. currently the CREM (Computing Resource Management) is a subcommittee.

The board usually meets every other week. It reports to the ATLAS executive board and is chaired by the deputy spokespersons (current Anna Sfyrla and Guillaume Unal).

Membership

Members are

  • Spokesperson + deputies
  • Physics coordinator + deputy
  • Data preparation coordinator + deputy
  • Computing coordinator + deputy
  • Trigger coordinator + deputy
  • Run coordinator + deputy
  • Software coordinators
  • ADC coordinators
  • Simulation conveners
  • MC production coordinators
  • Physics validation coordinators
  • Chairs of any subcommittees (e.g. CREM)

Meetings

OAB meetings are generally scheduled fortnightly, Mondays at 10:30 in this indico category. Minutes of each meeting are attached to the agenda. A convenient summary of meeting agendas and links to minutes is available via indicomb.

Watch list

The OAB follows important issues through a watch list. Its content is available on indico and updated for every meeting.
